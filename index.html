Who I Am

A little About Me

So I started out studying electronics and communication engineering in India, fully convinced I was heading somewhere with circuits and signals. That did not happen. Somewhere between debugging code at midnight and getting weirdly obsessed with why a data pipeline was failing silently, I realised data engineering and AI was where I actually wanted to be. Not exactly the plan, but here we are and I am not complaining.

Right now I am doing my master's in Data Analytics Engineering at Northeastern University and on co-op at Medlaunch Concepts, where I am building the data backbone of a healthcare AI platform. That mostly means taking clinical accreditation data that was clearly never designed to be machine-readable, and somehow making it useful enough for a model to trust. It is messy, it is frustrating, and it is genuinely one of the more interesting problems I have worked on.

What actually drives me is the curiosity side of this field. Every few months there is a new tool, a new framework, a new way of thinking about a problem, and I genuinely get excited about that rather than overwhelmed by it. I like picking something up, breaking it, figuring out how it works, and then finding a real problem to apply it to. That itch to keep learning is probably what pushed me from electronics into data into ML into GenAI, and I do not see it stopping anytime soon.

Outside of work I am big on spending time with friends and family, whether that is a game night, a home-cooked dinner, or just a good night out. I work out regularly and take long walks with good music or a Mel Robbins podcast, which somehow always fixes whatever was wrong.
